Neural Networks Literature Review

An ethical investigation into Spotify recommendations

Becky Zhang and Alex Franklin | February 25, 2021

  1. https://web.stanford.edu/~rezab/nips2014workshop/submits/logmat.pdf

    2. This Stanford research paper by a Spotify engineer presents logistic matrix factorization as a tool for collaborative filtering, one of the three deep learning tools Spotify uses to make recommendations based on implicit user feedback data. This offers a more concrete explanation of one way to effectively conduct collaborative filtering.

  2. https://dl.acm.org/doi/abs/10.1145/3366423.3380281?casa_token=vDRONauZb0oAAAAA:7WQ_BxL-nXgJPL4d2jPLyL9N9avIMfEWW5iJ7roEMjDBN7SxPQHfm0kspK_LuBipr9x7N9Yi4p90YQ

    3. This research article highlights a key flaw to collaborative filtering models: their reliance on existing implicit user data generates recommendations biased toward older and more popular songs, compromising and actively reducing the diversity of music which people using their recommendations consume. We haven’t been able to read past the abstract, however, because we don’t yet have access to the full paper.

  3. https://benanne.github.io/2014/08/05/spotify-cnns.html

    4. Spotify uses a combination of collaborative filtering, NLP, and content-based recommendation (which this post is about). The audio signal of one million songs on Spotify was used to train a neural network, of which different convolutional layers seem to recognize certain aspects of the music.

  4. https://blogs.commons.georgetown.edu/cctp-607-spring2019/2019/05/06/music-to-my-ears-de-blackboxing-spotifys-recommendation-algorithm/

    5. This post had a general overview of Spotify and its Discover Weekly feature. It went into additional detail about the issues of the “cold start problem” and outlier detection in recommendation decision making processes.

  5. https://blogs.cornell.edu/info2040/2018/09/17/how-does-spotify-know-you-so-well/

    6. This is another more general overview of Spotify’s deep learning processes, with some helpful diagrams and explanations of such things as “cultural vectors” defined using NLP and user and song vectors and their matrix operations in collaborative filtering. It also mentions Spotify’s acquisition of music intelligence company The Echo Nest, which was central to its initial success in generating superior recommendations.
